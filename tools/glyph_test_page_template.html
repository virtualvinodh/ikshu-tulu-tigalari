Every consonant against every vowel sign (36 × 18: inherent-vowel column, 13 vowel signs, Candrabindu/Anusvara/Visarga, and Halant/virama) - not limited to combinations with a dedicated ligature glyph. EE/AI (reorder before the base) and OO/AU (split before+after the base) are shaped entirely by HarfBuzz's own built-in Tigalari support, not this font's GSUB - so this table also doubles as a check on that.

Every consonant against every consonant (36 × 36, including the diagonal - geminate clusters like "kka" are real conjuncts too), typed as C1 + conjoiner + C2 - the same shape our akhn GSUB rules match on. Only a fraction of these 1296 pairs have a dedicated ligature glyph; this table shows at a glance which fall back to unligated default rendering instead.

Every independent vowel × the two Vedic tone marks we have glyphs for ("svaras" - Svarita and Anudatta; Udatta is conventionally left unmarked), and every consonant × all 4 Repha/Svarita/ Anudatta/Gemination marks.

Real Sanskrit Buddhist/Vedic texts (Heart Sutra, dharanis, Lotus Sutra, Lalitavistara, Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta, a Vedic passage) - the same texts satisarsharada's own test page uses, sourced from its Sharada-script text and mechanically transliterated (see brahmic_translit.py) rather than retyped. Main line is Tigalari; the small annotation above each word can show Kannada, IAST, or both.
